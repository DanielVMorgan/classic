Colophon

This site was designed to evoke the look and feel of Classic Mac OS System 1.0 through 7.0 and HyperCard when the graphics were 1-bit black and white pixels. Most of the navigation icons—the pointing hands, home button, mail button, category icons—are vector recreations that will always appear as 1-bit icons at any size. The fonts are converted from the original bitmap system fonts into modern TrueType (.ttf) fonts and web-optimized .woff and .woff2 file types.

One of the biggest challenges was getting access to the classic 1-bit fonts and converting them to web-optimized fonts. In the spirit of a classic colophon here are the fonts used:

Chicago for the menus and rounded buttons, Espy Sans for icon labels and text, Espy Bold for subheadings, and Palatino for major page headings. I've posted these fonts and recreated 1-bit icons at my repo: Classic Mac Elements.

This project wouldn't have been possible without a large community of retro Macintosh enthusiasts freely sharing their discoveries, workflows, and files. Here's a non-comprehensive list:

HyperCard Simulator, Infinite Mac, Macintosh Garden, Macintosh Repository, Classic Mac Font Repo, Matt Kane's site for inspiration, dfontSplitter by Peter Upfold, and FontLab 8.

And finally special thanks to the godparents of the Macintosh and HyperCard look and feel,
Bill Atkinson and Susan Kare.
